My Career Interests

Data Science

As a physics major, I've always had an insatiable thirst for knowledge about how nature works at a fundamental level. This consequently drew my attention to data science, as the mediums we use to model nature's functionality stems from acquiring and analyzing experimental data. Now, I have found a love for data analysis of systems beyond just nature and physics. Give me a data set from any system, such as unnatural ones such as financial systems, and I would enjoy analyzing it and drawing interesting conclusions about the system it was pulled from.

Software Engineering

The internet has gifted me with so much knowledge, including that to build this very website, and that's all thanks to software engineering. I am interested in full stack software engineering as I enjoy data analysis and the creative/aesthetic power programming holds. I would love to build software that can help make life a little easier for people.

Machine Learning

Interlinked with the other two interests, I am fascinated by the potential machine learning holds. Coming from a physics background, I've witnessed the power that machine learning and artificial intelligence has on frontier particle physics research. I plan on learning and thinking of new ways on how it can be used in other fields too, as datasets on any system has potential machine learning applications.

My Projects

Critical Phenomena: Ising Model

First college project. Studied thermodynamics and statistical mechanics to implement Monte-Carlo simulations on a multi-dimensional Ising Model with a very large amount of spin sites (would take years for exact calculation) in order to obtain critical temperatures.

Hadron Monitor Analysis

Imported raw data from Fermilab's Hadron Monitor on their NuMi beam. Recieved data on various physical parameters with column sizes of 1.5 million indices. Converted to CSV and utilized PANDAS and matplotlib to clean, format, and analyze data in order to draw important physical conclusions about the functionality of the particle beam.

Higgs Test

Implemented MadGraph software and studied aspects of quantum field theory to simulate the 2012 CERN discovery of the Higgs Boson. Analyzed 10 runs of 10,000 proton-proton collision events and utilized python to correlate four-momenta of post-collision particles with respect to their event density and discovered the Higgs particle resonance peak.

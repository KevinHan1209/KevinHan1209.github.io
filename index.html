My Passions

Physics

As a physics major, I've always had an insatiable thirst for knowledge about how nature works at a fundamental level. I remember the feeling of being able to visualize the world around me in terms of force and energy diagrams after taking my first physics class in high school, and it added an entire new dimension of enjoyment to my life. Now, after having learned about quantum mechanics and general relativity, that visualization has become much more difficult but I still like to go back to the blissful ignorance of my roots:). Jokes aside, through first-principles reasoning, physics has helped me in every other aspect of my life in ways I couldn't have imagined.

Mathematics

Behind all scientific models are fundamental patterns of nature, the language of the universe. These patterns, manifested through the foundations of logic and set theory, make up modern mathematics. I am deeply fascinated with both applied mathematics such as linear algebra as well as pure mathematics with abstract algebras. As a physicist though, I am always looking for ways to transition pure mathematics into application.

Artificial Intelligence

Intertwined with the other two passions, they inevitably drew me towards artificial intelligence, as what is a coherent understanding of the universe without an investigation into how intelligent beings interact with it's environments? I am mainly interested in deep reinforcement learning algorithms, as this most closely mimics how human beings learn. I seek ways to utilize our understanding of the universe as well as patterns within such as fundamental symmetries to improve SotA algorithms in reinforcement learning.

My Projects

Human-Inspired Exploration in Reinforcement Learning

Collaborated with NYU's department of psychology to study how infants explore their environments compared to state of the art unsupervised reinforcement learning algorithms, such as APT, DIAYN, and RND. Also built RL environment matching real-world experiments as well as post-processed experiment data to a MDP formalism.

Federated Ensembled Reinforcement Learning on Micro-UAVs

Created a universal platform for ensembled and federated deep reinforcement learning compatible with stable_baselines3 and implemented a novel, momentum-based federated RL algorithm, FedSVRPG-M, along with other state-of-the-art algorithms in an ensembled fashion for small quadrotor drones.

Higgs Test

Implemented MadGraph software and studied aspects of quantum field theory to simulate the 2012 CERN discovery of the Higgs Boson. Analyzed 10 runs of 10,000 proton-proton collision events and utilized python to correlate four-momenta of post-collision particles with respect to their event density and discovered the Higgs particle resonance peak.
